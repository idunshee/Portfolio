Mapping Cancer Data

The purpose of this project is to understand the relationship between lung cancer deaths, health factors, and demographics. All maps were created from data provided by County Health Rankings, The U.S. Department of Health and Human Services, National Institutes of Health, National Cancer Institute, and The University of Iowa Department of Geographical and Sustainability Sciences. The data was mapped based upon the standard deviation of the datasets, and the color schemes were developed by Color Brewer developed byCynthia Brewer, Mark Harrower, and The Pennsylvania State University.

Mapping Cancer Data: Smoking Percent

The purpose of this map, like others in the Iowa Cancer Maps Group, is to better understand the relationships between certain health factors and cancer deaths. The contribution of this map is to depict the percent population of smokers in each of the Iowa counties.

Mapping Cancer Data: Health Care Cost

The purpose of this map, like others in the Iowa Cancer Maps Group, is to better understand the relationships between certain health factors and cancer deaths. The contribution of this map is to depict the average healthcare cost in each of the Iowa counties.

Mapping Cancer Data: Percent Age Over 65

The purpose of this map, like others in the Iowa Cancer Maps Group, is to better understand the relationships between certain health factors and cancer deaths. The contribution of this map is to depict the percent of the population over the age of 65 in each of the Iowa counties.

Mapping Cancer Data: Death Rate

The purpose of this map, like others in the Iowa Cancer Maps Group, is to better understand the relationships between certain health factors and cancer deaths. The contribution of this map is to depict the rate of cancer related deaths in each of the Iowa counties.
